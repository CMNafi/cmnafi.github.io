Games that changed how I think

Strategy, systems, and why good games can sharpen your model of the world.

Published January 27, 2026 7 min read
gamessystemsdesign

Games can be laboratories for judgment. They compress feedback, consequence, and uncertainty into a form you can feel.

They teach constraint

The best systems make tradeoffs legible. Every move closes one door and opens another.

They reward adaptation

A good game notices whether you are learning or just repeating a comfortable pattern.

They make invisible systems visible

Underneath every memorable game is a model of incentives, resources, timing, and risk.
