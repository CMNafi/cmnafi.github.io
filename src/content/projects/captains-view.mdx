**An interactive tactical viewport designed to calculate, validate, and mirror cricket fielding configurations on a premium virtual turf.**

---

## The Vision: Tactical Clarity on the Field

In the high-pressure environment of a cricket match, a captain’s ability to coordinate their fielders determines the outcome of the game. However, existing diagram tools are static, clumsy, or entirely unaware of active match rules. 

**Captains View** bridges this gap by providing an interactive digital board that understands the game. Built with premium **vanilla TypeScript** and native vector **SVG**, the application acts as an active strategizing instrument. It tracks every player’s position, calculates their distances from the pitch, and validates if the layout is match-legal.

---

## Core Capabilities

### 1. The Turf Canvas
Instead of flat, uninspiring clip-art, the fielding canvas displays a highly detailed top-down view of a modern cricket ground. The grass is styled in a deep, rich premium emerald green with subtle rings, contrasted by clean white crease markings, the 30-yard inner circle, and the outer boundary rope. Every coordinate is tracked relative to the pitch center, enabling responsive resizing across screens.

### 2. PointerEvents Drag Engine
We bypassed heavy frameworks to implement custom drag mechanics using standard web `PointerEvents`. This ensures lag-free movement of player tokens on both desktop mice and mobile touchscreens. When players are dragged:
- The **Wicket Keeper** and **Bowler** default-snap near the crease lines but can be adjusted for fine tactical tuning.
- Remaining fielders move freely within the boundary.
- Snapping and boundaries are verified in real-time on `pointerup`.

### 3. Euclidean Rules Engine
The core rules engine monitors state continuously:
- **Active Roster**: Ensures exactly 11 active players are placed on the field, with the 12th man positioned on the bench.
- **Role Verification**: Confirms that exactly one active Bowler is running in and exactly one Wicket Keeper is positioned behind the stumps.
- **Powerplay Radar**: When the Powerplay is active, the engine calculates the distance from the pitch midpoint for each fielder:
  
  `distance = sqrt((x - 50)² + (y - 50)²)`

  If a captain attempts to drop a third fielder outside the 30-yard circle during a Powerplay, the engine rejects the placement, snaps the player back, and flashes a diagnostic warning.

### 4. Batter Handedness Mirroring
When a left-handed batter (LHB) takes strike, the captain must quickly mirror the field. The application makes this instant. Clicking the LHB toggle applies a mathematical mirror reflection across the vertical axis of the pitch:

`x_mirrored = 100 - x`

All players transition smoothly to their opposite positions (e.g., a Point fielder automatically moves to Square Leg), saving valuable strategizing time.
