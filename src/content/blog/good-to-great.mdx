Jim Collins gave the business world the flywheel and Level 5 leadership, which sounds dry until you realize he is really arguing for disciplined compounding over corporate theater. I am very much in favor of less theater.

## What stayed with me

What stayed was the language around momentum. The flywheel idea is useful because it makes success feel operational. Great outcomes are often the cumulative result of repeated, unglamorous consistency.

## What it changed

It changed how I evaluate organizations. I started looking less at vision statements and more at whether there is coherent follow-through: people, process, timing, and clarity moving in the same direction.

## Why I still keep it on the shelf

I still think of this book whenever an organization seems to confuse energy with alignment. Good to Great keeps reminding me that durability usually comes from structure, not adrenaline.
