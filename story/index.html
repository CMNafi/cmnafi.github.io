THE SHORT ANSWER

I'm a solutions engineer at Dasseti, working at the intersection of institutional capital and the data infrastructure that moves it. I grew up in Bangladesh, left home at sixteen on a full scholarship to a United World College in China, and landed in a town of eight thousand in Iowa for university. I've since worked with BlackRock, Mayo Clinic, and Skybridge Associates — three very different institutions that share a single truth: the answer is almost always in the data, and the edge is almost always in how quickly you can get to it. I build tools, I ship writing, and I try to keep the feedback loop short.

QUALIFYING ROUND

Bangladesh → China → Iowa → Tampa

I grew up in Dinajpur, Bangladesh, in a household that treated reading as the default answer to boredom. At sixteen I left on a full scholarship to UWC Changshu (China), moving to a country where I didn't speak the language and learning most of what I know about self-reliance in the space of two years.

University took me to Luther College in Decorah, Iowa — a town of eight thousand people where winter teaches you patience and a small campus teaches you that depth beats breadth if you let it. I majored in Data Science and minored in Computer Science, and graduated 2022.

I now live in Tampa, Florida, which is where the F1, the cricket, and the work all happen to sit.

What the route gave me: the instinct to read a system top-down and bottom-up at the same time, comfort with being the newest person in the room, and a bias for building the tool I wish existed instead of complaining about the one that doesn't.

CURRENT LAP

Day job is Dasseti. Outside that, I'm building a set of public finance data tools at cmnafi.com/garage — most notably the ADV Screener, a 13F Data Finder, and a manager intelligence dashboard that ties entity resolution, regulatory filings, and narrative summarization together. They're works in progress on purpose.

I also write one or two Field Notes a week on cmnafi.com/field-notes covering the corners of institutional finance, venture capital, and market structure that I find most worth explaining. The writing and the tools feed each other — every post surfaces something worth turning into a tool, and every tool surfaces something worth writing about.
